Critical distinctions

  • FIGURE vs PATTERN. A pattern is just an interval sequence (e.g. '33', '333', '434'). A figure is a pattern with a starting-degree prefix (e.g. '133' = start on degree 1, then intervals 3,3). In the HarpChords table, row labels are bare patterns. Everywhere else, strings like '133' or '5333' are full figures.
  • Two figures producing the same chord is normal. Pattern 233 and pattern 434 give identical pitch-class sets at every starting degree — 434 is just 233 with its middle note displaced up one octave. The handout lists both because they are different physical fingerings.
  • CW vs CCW. Each cycle is a closed loop of 7 diatonic chords that starts and ends at I. CW and CCW are the two directions of traversal. Every edge has a CW-label AND a CCW-label — these describe the mood of the same edge in each direction.

Figure parsing

A figure is a string of characters from the interval alphabet. The FIRST character is the starting scale degree; each SUBSEQUENT character is the interval (inclusive) from the previous finger to the next finger.

span = 1 + Σ(interval − 1) per interval; intervals are inclusive on both ends.

FigureParseSpanFingersIn key of C
133start degree 1; then interval 3, then interval 353I (C-E-G)
933start degree 9 (i.e. scale-degree 2 one octave up); intervals 3, 353ii one octave up (D-F-A)
5333start degree 5; intervals 3, 3, 374V7 (G-B-D-F)
1233start degree 1; intervals 2, 3, 364IΔ1 (C-D-F-A read as Dm7/C)

LH / RH figure-pair notation

When two figures appear together (e.g. '133 933' or '433 A43'), the LEFT figure is the Left Hand (LH) and the RIGHT figure is the Right Hand (RH).

PairMeaning
133 933LH=I(str 1), RH=ii(str 9)
433 A43LH=IV(str 4), RH=vi¹(str 10)
5333 D43LH=V7(str 5), RH=ii¹(str 13)

Non-diatonic marker (—): A dash (—) in the HarpChords table means non-diatonic: that pattern starting on that degree produces a chord outside the key's diatonic set.

Diatonic cycles (3)

Each cycle is a closed loop of 7 diatonic chords starting and ending at I. CW and CCW are the two directions of traversal; every edge has a CW mood label and a CCW mood label.
