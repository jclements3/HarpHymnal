# Drills

Sequences for practicing the encoding under the hand. Every drill below
runs with `Tonic: C` unless otherwise noted; transpose by changing the
header. Shapes within a row are **space-separated**. Where a row gets
long, the `&` and `$` operators expand a single shape into a run.

Standard header for any drill below:

```
Title: <drill name>
Tonic: C
---
<row of shapes>
```

The drills are pure shape-setup sequences. Rhythm and articulation are
the player’s interpretation — the grid below tells the hand what to set,
not when to strike.

-----

## 1 · Diatonic triads — root, 1st inv, 2nd inv (3 × 7)

**1. Root-position triads** — `3x^133 3x^233 3x^333 3x^433 3x^533 3x^633 3x^733`
All 7 degrees in root position. Mode-color tour: I, ii, iii, IV, V, vi, vii°.

**2. 1st-inversion triads (3rd in bass)** — `3x^334 3x^434 3x^534 3x^634 3x^734 3x^134 3x^234`
Same 7 triads, intervals 3+4. Lifted feel; common in walking-bass reharm.

**3. 2nd-inversion triads (5th in bass)** — `3x^543 3x^643 3x^743 3x^143 3x^243 3x^343 3x^443`
Same 7 triads, intervals 4+3. Cadential/passing texture; less stable.

## 2 · Diatonic 7ths — all 4 inversions (4 × 7)

**4. Root position** — `3x^1333 3x^2333 3x^3333 3x^4333 3x^5333 3x^6333 3x^7333`
All 7 diatonic 7ths root: Imaj7, ii7, iii7, IVmaj7, V7, vi7, viiø7.

**5. 1st inversion (3rd in bass)** — `3x^3332 3x^4332 3x^5332 3x^6332 3x^7332 3x^1332 3x^2332`
Half-step bite at top (interval 2 = 2nd between 7th and root).

**6. 2nd inversion (5th in bass)** — `3x^5323 3x^6323 3x^7323 3x^1323 3x^2323 3x^3323 3x^4323`
2nd in the middle. Open-sounding; common in two-hand reharm.

**7. 3rd inversion (7th in bass)** — `3x^7233 3x^1233 3x^2233 3x^3233 3x^4233 3x^5233 3x^6233`
7th in bass — strong downward pull. Voice-leading workhorse.

## 3 · Universal-winner open voicings on every degree

**8. Pattern `335` modal cycle** — `3x^1335 3x^2335 3x^3335 3x^4335 3x^5335 3x^6335 3x^7335`
Same shape recoloured seven ways. Repeat with `344`, `346`, `355`, `356`, etc.

**9. Pattern `346` octave climb** — `1x^1346&5`
The 346 open tetrad re-stamped in octaves 1 through 6. `&` expands one shape into a vertical climb.

**10. Pattern `456` modal cycle, octave 3** — `3x^1456 3x^2456 3x^3456 3x^4456 3x^5456 3x^6456 3x^7456`
Wide-spread universal-winner tour.

## 4 · Sus and quartal alternates

**11. sus4 cycle** — `3x^142 3x^242 3x^342 3x^442 3x^542 3x^642 3x^742`
Triad with 4 instead of 3 (intervals 4+2 — root, 4th, 5th). Csus4 = C-F-G, etc.

**12. Quartal triad cycle** — `3x^144 3x^244 3x^344 3x^444 3x^544 3x^644 3x^744`
Two stacked 4ths. Mode 4 (`3x^444`) is the Lydian quartal — special.

**13. sus2 cycle (add9-no-3)** — `3x^124 3x^224 3x^324 3x^424 3x^524 3x^624 3x^724`
Root + 2 + 5 (intervals 2+4). Csus2 = C-D-G. Open, ambiguous color.

## 5 · Two-hand textures — LH bass + RH tetrad

These use the **separate-shapes** form: each chord is two consecutive
shapes, the first pinned to LH (`L`), the second to RH (`R`).

**14. Pool walk, bass + 7th chord** — `1L^1 3R^1333 1L^2 3R^2333 1L^3 3R^3333 1L^4 3R^4333 1L^5 3R^5333 1L^6 3R^6333 1L^7 3R^7333`
Low bass + 7th-chord tetrad on each degree. Standard reharm two-hand texture.

**15. Slash-bass walk under stable I** — `1L^1 3R^1333 1L^2 3R^1333 1L^3 3R^1333 1L^5 3R^1333`
Bass walks 1-2-3-5 under unchanging Cmaj7. Pedal effect; non-functional bass.

**16. Open voicing two-hand** — `1L^1 3R^1346 1L^4 3R^4346 1L^5 3R^5346 1L^1 3R^1346`
I-IV-V-I with the universal-winner `1346`. Modern open feel.

## 6 · Voice-leading cycles

**17. Cycle of 4ths through 7ths** — `3x^1333 3x^4333 3x^7333 3x^3333 3x^6333 3x^2333 3x^5333 3x^1333`
I-IV-vii°-iii-vi-ii-V-I. Each move up a 4th; common tones drop a step.

**18. Cycle of 3rds through 7ths** — `3x^1333 3x^3333 3x^5333 3x^7333 3x^2333 3x^4333 3x^6333 3x^1333`
I-iii-V-vii°-ii-IV-vi-I. Two common tones each move. Smooth, parallel.

**19. ii-V-I cliché** — `3x^2333 3x^5333 3x^1333`
The reharm-cliché cadence. Practice transposed by changing `Tonic:`.

**20. vi-ii-V-I (jazz turnaround)** — `3x^6333 3x^2333 3x^5333 3x^1333`
Diatonic doo-wop. The 7th-chord pool’s most-used progression.

## 7 · Self-similar climbs (`$` operator)

`$N` repeats the **last digit** of the shape N more times, chaining shape
by shape. Use it for all-thirds, all-fourths, etc.

**21. Stacked-thirds chain** — `1x^1333$3`
Expands to `1x^1333 3 3 3` — third-stack tetrad C1-E1-G1-B1, then D2-F2-A2-C3, then E3-G3-B3-D4, then F4-A4-C5-E5. Hand-spread workhorse.

**22. Quartal chain** — `1x^14$5`
Expands to `1x^14 4 4 4 4 4`. Six dyads, each a 4th up from the prior. C-F, F-B, B-E, …

**23. Stacked-thirds full sweep** — `1x^1333$~`
Climb the third-stack chain to the top of the harp, then mirror back down. The canonical reach drill.

**24. Stacked-thirds octave climb** — `1x^1333&3`
The whole tetrad re-stamped in octaves 1-4. Compare with drill 21 — same notes, different shape sequence.

## 8 · Subscript assembly studies

Subscripts (`₁`–`₈`) mark **assembly order**: when each finger plants in
the air-set sequence. Position in the digit string still determines
which finger plays which string (1st = ring, 2nd = middle, 3rd = index,
4th = thumb).

**25. Block strike** — `1x^1₁3₁3₁3₁`
All four fingers plant together. One coordinated set; cadence/opening texture.

**26. Bottom-up roll (default order, written out)** — `1x^1₁3₂3₃3₄`
Ring → middle → index → thumb. Standard arpeggiated assembly.

**27. Top-down roll (anchor apex)** — `1x^1₄3₃3₂3₁`
Thumb first (anchor melody), ring last. Arrival chords with melody on top.

**28. Bottom-dyad + top-dyad** — `1x^1₁3₁3₂3₂`
Ring+middle plant first, then index+thumb. Frame the bass, then add the top.

**29. Outer pair + inner pair** — `1x^1₁3₂3₂3₁`
Ring+thumb (outer) first, then middle+index (inner). Frame-and-fill.

**30. Inner pair + outer pair** — `1x^1₂3₁3₁3₂`
Middle+index (inner) first, ring+thumb (outer) wraps around.

**31. Top-3 + bass arrives last** — `1x^1₂3₁3₁3₁`
Top three together, then ring drops in. Drop-1 effect; cadential bass arrival.

**32. Bass + top-3 cluster** — `1x^1₁3₂3₂3₂`
Ring alone, then top three together. Bass-then-chord; classic accompaniment.

**33. Skip-finger interlock** — `1x^1₁3₂3₁3₂`
Ring+index (₁), then middle+thumb (₂). Two interleaved dyads.

## 9 · Modal & relative-minor sweeps

**34. Aeolian-tonic phrase** — `3x^6333 3x^2333 3x^5333 3x^6333`
vi-ii-V-vi: tonicizes A-minor while staying in C-major’s diatonic pool.

**35. Modal reharm sweep** — `3x^6333 3x^4333 3x^6333 3x^2333 3x^5333 3x^6333`
vi-IV-vi-ii-V-vi. Aeolian centre with Ionian-labelled chords.

## 10 · Dyad cycles — idiomatic harp intervals

**36. 3rds on every degree** — `3x^13 3x^23 3x^33 3x^43 3x^53 3x^63 3x^73`
Two-finger 3rd on each scale degree. Ring+middle, comfortable reach.

**37. 5ths on every degree** — `3x^15 3x^25 3x^35 3x^45 3x^55 3x^65 3x^75`
Open 5ths. Ring+thumb territory. The open-fifth bell sound on each degree.

**38. Octaves on every degree** — `3x^18 3x^28 3x^38 3x^48 3x^58 3x^68 3x^78`
Octave doubling on each degree. Ring+thumb, comfortable octave grip.

**39. 10ths on every degree** — `3x^1a 3x^2a 3x^3a 3x^4a 3x^5a 3x^6a 3x^7a`
Wide 10ths. Ring+thumb at full stretch. Bass-melody framing.

## 11 · Approach + cadential pairs

**40. Authentic cadence (V7→I)** — `3x^5333 3x^1333`
G7→Cmaj7. Strongest diatonic resolution. Drill in every key by changing `Tonic:`.

**41. Plagal cadence (IV→I)** — `3x^4333 3x^1333`
Fmaj7→Cmaj7. The “amen” cadence. Common at hymn endings.

**42. Deceptive resolution (V→vi)** — `3x^5333 3x^6333`
G7→Am7. Substitute for V→I.

**43. Half-cadence (IV→V)** — `3x^4333 3x^5333`
Fmaj7→G7. Mid-phrase pause; sets up the next phrase to resolve.

**44. Extended cadence (IV→V→I)** — `3x^4333 3x^5333 3x^1333`
Three-step cadential close. The hymn-ending workhorse.

## 12 · Pedal-point textures

**45. Tonic pedal under chord cycle** — `1L^1 3R^1333 1L^1 3R^4333 1L^1 3R^5333 1L^1 3R^1333`
LH C1 sustained throughout, RH cycles I-IV-V-I. Tonic-pedal effect.

**46. Dominant pedal under cadence** — `1L^5 3R^2333 1L^5 3R^5333 1L^5 3R^1333`
G1 pedal under ii-V-I. Tension/release; classic Bach idiom.

## 13 · Common-tone voice-leading pairs

**47. I→vi (3 common tones)** — `3x^1333 3x^6333`
Cmaj7→Am7. Share C-E-G; only B→A moves. Smoothest possible move.

**48. IV→ii (3 common tones)** — `3x^4333 3x^2333`
Fmaj7→Dm7. Share F-A-C; only E→D moves. Equal smoothness.

**49. iii→I (3 common tones)** — `3x^3333 3x^1333`
Em7→Cmaj7. Share E-G-B; only D→C moves. Voice-leading-first cliché.

## 14 · Sus-resolution drills

**50. sus4→3 on V** — `3x^542 3x^533`
Gsus4 (G-C-D) resolves to G triad (G-B-D). Cadential 6-4 disguise.

**51. sus4→3 cycle, every degree** — `3x^142 3x^133 3x^242 3x^233 3x^342 3x^333 3x^442 3x^433 3x^542 3x^533 3x^642 3x^633 3x^742 3x^733`
Seven paired suspensions: each sus4 (4+2) resolves to its triad (3+3). Hymn-cadence idiom.

## 15 · Hymn-phrase shape templates

**52. Basic 4-bar (I-IV-V-I)** — `1L^1 3R^1333 1L^4 3R^4333 1L^5 3R^5333 1L^1 3R^1333`
The hymn workhorse. Two-hand bass+tetrad.

**53. Antecedent-consequent (8 bar)** — `1L^1 3R^1333 1L^4 3R^4333 1L^2 3R^2333 1L^5 3R^5333 1L^1 3R^1333 1L^6 3R^6333 1L^5 3R^5333 1L^1 3R^1333`
A: I-IV-ii-V (half-cadence). B: I-vi-V-I (authentic). The classic hymn pair.

**54. Doo-wop hymn (I-vi-IV-V-I)** — `1L^1 3R^1333 1L^6 3R^6333 1L^4 3R^4333 1L^5 3R^5333 1L^1 3R^1333`
50s-style cycle. Reharm cliché when the original hymn is plain I-V-I.

**55. Pachelbel descent (I-V-vi-iii-IV-I-IV-V)** — `1L^1 3R^1333 1L^5 3R^5333 1L^6 3R^6333 1L^3 3R^3333 1L^4 3R^4333 1L^1 3R^1333 1L^4 3R^4333 1L^5 3R^5333`
Bass walks down by step. Voice-leading workshop disguised as a progression.

**56. Suspended amen (IV-Vsus-V-I)** — `1L^4 3R^4333 1L^5 3R^542 1L^5 3R^533 1L^1 3R^1333`
Gsus4 (G-C-D) suspends, resolves to G triad, lands on I. Hymn-ending classic.
